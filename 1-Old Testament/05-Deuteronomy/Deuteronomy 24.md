# Deuteronomy 24 / 申命记 24

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | When a man hath taken a wife, and married her, and it come to pass that she find no favour in his eyes, because he hath found some uncleanness in her: then let him write her a bill of divorcement, and give it in her hand, and send her out of his house. |
| 中文 | 人若娶妻以后，见她有什么不合理的事，不喜悦她，就可以写休书交在她手中，打发她离开夫家。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | And when she is departed out of his house, she may go and be another man's wife. |
| 中文 | 妇人离开夫家以后，可以去嫁别人。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | And if the latter husband hate her, and write her a bill of divorcement, and giveth it in her hand, and sendeth her out of his house; or if the latter husband die, which took her to be his wife; |
| 中文 | 后夫若恨恶她，写休书交在她手中，打发她离开夫家，或是娶她为妻的后夫死了， |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Her former husband, which sent her away, may not take her again to be his wife, after that she is defiled; for that is abomination before the LORD: and thou shalt not cause the land to sin, which the LORD thy God giveth thee for an inheritance. |
| 中文 | 打发她去的前夫不可在妇人玷污之后再娶她为妻，因为这是耶和华所憎恶的。不可使耶和华你神所赐为业之地被玷污了。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | When a man hath taken a new wife, he shall not go out to war, neither shall he be charged with any business: but he shall be free at home one year, and shall cheer up his wife which he hath taken. |
| 中文 | 新娶妻之人不可从军出征，也不可托他办理什么公事，可以在家清闲一年，使他所娶的妻快活。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | No man shall take the nether or the upper millstone to pledge: for he taketh a man's life to pledge. |
| 中文 | 不可拿人的全盘磨石或是上磨石作当头，因为这是拿人的命作当头。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If a man be found stealing any of his brethren of the children of Israel, and maketh merchandise of him, or selleth him; then that thief shall die; and thou shalt put evil away from among you. |
| 中文 | 若遇见人拐带以色列中的一个弟兄，当奴才待他，或是卖了他，那拐带人的就必治死。这样，便将那恶从你们中间除掉。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Take heed in the plague of leprosy, that thou observe diligently, and do according to all that the priests the Levites shall teach you: as I commanded them, so ye shall observe to do. |
| 中文 | 在大麻疯的灾病上，你们要谨慎，照祭司利未人一切所指教你们的留意遵行。我怎样吩咐他们，你们要怎样遵行。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Remember what the LORD thy God did unto Miriam by the way, after that ye were come forth out of Egypt. |
| 中文 | 当记念出埃及后，在路上，耶和华你神向米利暗所行的事。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | When thou dost lend thy brother any thing, thou shalt not go into his house to fetch his pledge. |
| 中文 | 你借给邻舍，不拘是什么，不可进他家拿他的当头。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Thou shalt stand abroad, and the man to whom thou dost lend shall bring out the pledge abroad unto thee. |
| 中文 | 要站在外面，等那向你借贷的人把当头拿出来交给你。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | And if the man be poor, thou shalt not sleep with his pledge: |
| 中文 | 他若是穷人，你不可留他的当头过夜。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | In any case thou shalt deliver him the pledge again when the sun goeth down, that he may sleep in his own raiment, and bless thee: and it shall be righteousness unto thee before the LORD thy God. |
| 中文 | 日落的时候，总要把当头还他，使他用那件衣服盖着睡觉，他就为你祝福。这在耶和华你神面前就是你的义了。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Thou shalt not oppress an hired servant that is poor and needy, whether he be of thy brethren, or of thy strangers that are in thy land within thy gates: |
| 中文 | 困苦穷乏的雇工，无论是你的弟兄或是在你城里寄居的，你不可欺负他。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | At his day thou shalt give him his hire, neither shall the sun go down upon it; for he is poor, and setteth his heart upon it: lest he cry against thee unto the LORD, and it be sin unto thee. |
| 中文 | 要当日给他工价，不可等到日落。因为他穷苦，把心放在工价上，恐怕他因你求告耶和华，罪便归你了。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The fathers shall not be put to death for the children, neither shall the children be put to death for the fathers: every man shall be put to death for his own sin. |
| 中文 | 不可因子杀父，也不可因父杀子。凡被杀的都为本身的罪。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Thou shalt not pervert the judgment of the stranger, nor of the fatherless; nor take a widow's raiment to pledge: |
| 中文 | 你不可向寄居的和孤儿屈枉正直，也不可拿寡妇的衣裳作当头。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | But thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in Egypt, and the LORD thy God redeemed thee thence: therefore I command thee to do this thing. |
| 中文 | 要记念你在埃及作过奴仆。耶和华你的神从那里将你救赎，所以我吩咐你这样行。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | When thou cuttest down thine harvest in thy field, and hast forgot a sheaf in the field, thou shalt not go again to fetch it: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow: that the LORD thy God may bless thee in all the work of thine hands. |
| 中文 | 你在田间收割庄稼，若忘下一捆，不可回去再取，要留给寄居的与孤儿寡妇。这样，耶和华你神必在你手里所办的一切事上赐福与你。 |
