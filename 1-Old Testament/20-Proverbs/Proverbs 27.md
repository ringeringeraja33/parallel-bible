# Proverbs 27 / 箴言 27

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Boast not thyself of to morrow; for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth. |
| 中文 | 不要为明日自夸，因为一日要生何事，你尚且不能知道。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; a stranger, and not thine own lips. |
| 中文 | 要别人夸奖你，不可用口自夸。等外人称赞你，不可用嘴自称。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A stone is heavy, and the sand weighty; but a fool's wrath is heavier than them both. |
| 中文 | 石头重，沙土沉，愚妄人的恼怒，比这两样更重。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wrath is cruel, and anger is outrageous; but who is able to stand before envy? |
| 中文 | 忿怒为残忍，怒气为狂澜，惟有嫉妒，谁能敌得住呢。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Open rebuke is better than secret love. |
| 中文 | 当面的责备，强如背地的爱情。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Faithful are the wounds of a friend; but the kisses of an enemy are deceitful. |
| 中文 | 朋友加的伤痕，出于忠诚，仇敌连连亲嘴，却是多馀。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The full soul loatheth an honeycomb; but to the hungry soul every bitter thing is sweet. |
| 中文 | 人吃饱了，厌恶蜂房的蜜。人饥饿了，一切苦物都觉甘甜。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As a bird that wandereth from her nest, so is a man that wandereth from his place. |
| 中文 | 人离本处飘流，好像雀鸟离窝游飞。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Ointment and perfume rejoice the heart: so doth the sweetness of a man's friend by hearty counsel. |
| 中文 | 膏油与香料，使人心喜悦。朋友诚实的劝教，也是如此甘美。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Thine own friend, and thy father's friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother's house in the day of thy calamity: for better is a neighbour that is near than a brother far off. |
| 中文 | 你的朋友，和父亲的朋友，你都不可离弃。你遭难的日子，不要上弟兄的家去。向近的邻舍，强如远方的弟兄。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | My son, be wise, and make my heart glad, that I may answer him that reproacheth me. |
| 中文 | 我儿，你要作智慧人，好叫我的心喜欢，使我可以回答那讥笑我的人。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A prudent man foreseeth the evil, and hideth himself; but the simple pass on, and are punished. |
| 中文 | 通达人见祸藏躲。愚蒙人前往受害。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Take his garment that is surety for a stranger, and take a pledge of him for a strange woman. |
| 中文 | 谁为生人作保，就拿谁的衣服。谁为外女作保，谁就承当。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice, rising early in the morning, it shall be counted a curse to him. |
| 中文 | 清晨起来，大声给朋友祝福的，就算是咒诅他。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A continual dropping in a very rainy day and a contentious woman are alike. |
| 中文 | 大雨之日连连滴漏，和争吵的妇人一样。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Whosoever hideth her hideth the wind, and the ointment of his right hand, which bewrayeth itself. |
| 中文 | 想拦阻他的，便是拦阻风，也是右手抓油。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Iron sharpeneth iron; so a man sharpeneth the countenance of his friend. |
| 中文 | 铁磨铁，磨出刃来。朋友相感，（原文作磨朋友的脸）也是如此。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Whoso keepeth the fig tree shall eat the fruit thereof: so he that waiteth on his master shall be honoured. |
| 中文 | 看守无花果树的，必吃树上的果子。敬奉主人的，必得尊荣。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As in water face answereth to face, so the heart of man to man. |
| 中文 | 水中照脸，彼此相符。人与人，心也相对。 |

## 20

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |

| 中文 | 阴间和灭亡，永不满足。人的眼目，也是如此。 |

| --- | --- |
