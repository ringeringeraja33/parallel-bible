# Proverbs 25 / 箴言 25

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | These are also proverbs of Solomon, which the men of Hezekiah king of Judah copied out. |
| 中文 | 以下也是所罗门的箴言。是犹大王希西家的人所誊录的。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | It is the glory of God to conceal a thing: but the honour of kings is to search out a matter. |
| 中文 | 将事隐秘，乃神的荣耀。将事察清，乃君王的荣耀。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The heaven for height, and the earth for depth, and the heart of kings is unsearchable. |
| 中文 | 天之高，地之厚，君王之心也测不透。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Take away the dross from the silver, and there shall come forth a vessel for the finer. |
| 中文 | 除去银子的渣滓，就有银子出来，银匠能以作器皿。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Take away the wicked from before the king, and his throne shall be established in righteousness. |
| 中文 | 除去王面前的恶人，国位就靠公义坚立。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Put not forth thyself in the presence of the king, and stand not in the place of great men: |
| 中文 | 不要在王面前妄自尊大。不要在大人的位上站立。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For better it is that it be said unto thee, Come up hither; than that thou shouldest be put lower in the presence of the prince whom thine eyes have seen. |
| 中文 | 宁可有人说，请你上来，强如在你觐见的王子面前，叫你退下。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Go not forth hastily to strive, lest thou know not what to do in the end thereof, when thy neighbour hath put thee to shame. |
| 中文 | 不要冒失出去与人争竞，免得至终被他羞辱，你就不知道怎样行了。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Debate thy cause with thy neighbour himself; and discover not a secret to another: |
| 中文 | 你与邻舍争讼，要与他ㄧ人辩论。不可泄漏人的密事。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Lest he that heareth it put thee to shame, and thine infamy turn not away. |
| 中文 | 恐怕听见的人骂你，你的臭名就难以脱离。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A word fitly spoken is like apples of gold in pictures of silver. |
| 中文 | 一句话说得合宜，就如金苹果在银网子里。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As an earring of gold, and an ornament of fine gold, so is a wise reprover upon an obedient ear. |
| 中文 | 智慧人的劝戒，在顺从的人耳中，好像金耳环，和精金的妆饰。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As the cold of snow in the time of harvest, so is a faithful messenger to them that send him: for he refresheth the soul of his masters. |
| 中文 | 忠信的使者，叫差他的人心里舒畅，就如在收割时，有冰雪的凉气。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Whoso boasteth himself of a false gift is like clouds and wind without rain. |
| 中文 | 空夸赠送礼物的，好像无雨的风云。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | By long forbearing is a prince persuaded, and a soft tongue breaketh the bone. |
| 中文 | 恒常忍耐，可以劝动君王。柔和的舌头，能折断骨头。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Hast thou found honey? eat so much as is sufficient for thee, lest thou be filled therewith, and vomit it. |
| 中文 | 你得了蜜吗，只可吃够而已。恐怕你过饱就呕吐出来。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Withdraw thy foot from thy neighbour's house; lest he be weary of thee, and so hate thee. |
| 中文 | 你的脚要少进邻舍的家，恐怕他厌烦你，恨恶你。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A man that beareth false witness against his neighbour is a maul, and a sword, and a sharp arrow. |
| 中文 | 作假见证陷害邻舍的，就是大槌，是利刀，是快箭。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Confidence in an unfaithful man in time of trouble is like a broken tooth, and a foot out of joint. |
| 中文 | 患难时倚靠不忠诚的人，好像破坏的牙，错骨缝的脚。 |

## 20

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As he that taketh away a garment in cold weather, and as vinegar upon nitre, so is he that singeth songs to an heavy heart. |
| 中文 | 对伤心的人唱歌，就如冷天脱衣服，又如硷上倒醋。 |

## 21

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If thine enemy be hungry, give him bread to eat; and if he be thirsty, give him water to drink: |
| 中文 | 你的仇敌，若饿了就给他饭吃。若渴了就给他水喝。 |

## 22

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For thou shalt heap coals of fire upon his head, and the LORD shall reward thee. |
| 中文 | 因为你这样行，就是把炭火堆在他的头上。耶和华也必赏赐你。 |

## 23

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The north wind driveth away rain: so doth an angry countenance a backbiting tongue. |
| 中文 | 北风生雨，谗谤人的舌头也生怒容。 |

## 24

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | It is better to dwell in the corner of the housetop, than with a brawling woman and in a wide house. |
| 中文 | 宁可住在房顶的角上，不在宽阔的房屋与争吵的妇人同住。 |

## 25

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As cold waters to a thirsty soul, so is good news from a far country. |
| 中文 | 有好消息从远方来，就如拿凉水给口渴的人喝。 |

## 26

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A righteous man falling down before the wicked is as a troubled fountain, and a corrupt spring. |
| 中文 | 义人在恶人面前退缩，好像Y1浑之泉，弄浊之井。 |

## 27

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | It is not good to eat much honey: so for men to search their own glory is not glory. |
| 中文 | 吃蜜过多，是不好的。考究自己的荣耀，也是可厌的。 |

## 28

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that hath no rule over his own spirit is like a city that is broken down, and without walls. |
| 中文 | 人不制服自己的心，好像毁坏的城邑，没有墙垣。 |
