# Proverbs 14

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Every wise woman buildeth her house: but the foolish plucketh it down with her hands. |
| 中文 | 智慧妇人，建立家室。愚妄妇人，亲手拆毁。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that walketh in his uprightness feareth the LORD: but he that is perverse in his ways despiseth him. |
| 中文 | 行动正直的，敬畏耶和华。乖僻的，却藐视他。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | In the mouth of the foolish is a rod of pride: but the lips of the wise shall preserve them. |
| 中文 | 愚妄人口中骄傲，如杖责打己身。智慧人的嘴，必保守自己。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Where no oxen are, the crib is clean: but much increase is by the strength of the ox. |
| 中文 | 家里无牛，槽头乾净。土产加多，乃凭牛力。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A faithful witness will not lie: but a false witness will utter lies. |
| 中文 | 诚实见证人，不说谎话。假见证人，吐出谎言。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A scorner seeketh wisdom, and findeth it not: but knowledge is easy unto him that understandeth. |
| 中文 | 亵慢人寻智慧，却寻不着。聪明人易得知识。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Go from the presence of a foolish man, when thou perceivest not in him the lips of knowledge. |
| 中文 | 到愚昧人面前，不见他嘴中有知识。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The wisdom of the prudent is to understand his way: but the folly of fools is deceit. |
| 中文 | 通达人的智慧，在乎明白己道。愚昧人的愚妄，乃是诡诈。（或作自欺） |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Fools make a mock at sin: but among the righteous there is favour. |
| 中文 | 愚妄人犯罪，以为戏耍。（或作赎愆祭愚弄愚妄人）正直人互相喜悦。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The heart knoweth his own bitterness; and a stranger doth not intermeddle with his joy. |
| 中文 | 心中的苦楚，自己知道。心里的喜乐，外人无干。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The house of the wicked shall be overthrown: but the tabernacle of the upright shall flourish. |
| 中文 | 奸恶人的房屋必倾倒。正直人的帐棚必兴盛。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death. |
| 中文 | 有一条路人以为正。至终成为死亡之路。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Even in laughter the heart is sorrowful; and the end of that mirth is heaviness. |
| 中文 | 人在喜笑中，心也忧愁。快乐至极，就生愁苦。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The backslider in heart shall be filled with his own ways: and a good man shall be satisfied from himself. |
| 中文 | 心中背道的，必满得自己的结果。善人必从自己（的行为，得以知足） |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The simple believeth every word: but the prudent man looketh well to his going. |
| 中文 | 愚蒙人是话都信。通达人处处谨慎。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A wise man feareth, and departeth from evil: but the fool rageth, and is confident. |
| 中文 | 智慧人惧怕，就远离恶事。愚妄人却狂傲自恃。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that is soon angry dealeth foolishly: and a man of wicked devices is hated. |
| 中文 | 轻易发怒的，行事愚妄。设立诡计的，被人恨恶。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The simple inherit folly: but the prudent are crowned with knowledge. |
| 中文 | 愚蒙人得愚昧为产业。通达人得知识为冠冕。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The evil bow before the good; and the wicked at the gates of the righteous. |
| 中文 | 坏人俯伏在善人面前。恶人俯伏在义人门口。 |

## 20

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The poor is hated even of his own neighbour: but the rich hath many friends. |
| 中文 | 贫穷人连邻舍也恨他。富足人朋友最多。 |

## 21

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that despiseth his neighbour sinneth: but he that hath mercy on the poor, happy is he. |
| 中文 | 藐视邻舍的，这人有罪。怜悯贫穷的，这人有福。 |

## 22

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Do they not err that devise evil? but mercy and truth shall be to them that devise good. |
| 中文 | 谋恶的岂非走入迷途吗。谋善的必得慈爱和诚实。 |

## 23

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | In all labour there is profit: but the talk of the lips tendeth only to penury. |
| 中文 | 诸般勤劳，都有益处。嘴上多言，乃致穷乏。 |

## 24

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The crown of the wise is their riches: but the foolishness of fools is folly. |
| 中文 | 智慧人的财，为自己的冠冕。愚妄人的愚昧，终是愚昧。 |

## 25

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A true witness delivereth souls: but a deceitful witness speaketh lies. |
| 中文 | 作真见证的，救人性命。吐出谎言的，施行诡诈。 |

## 26

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | In the fear of the LORD is strong confidence: and his children shall have a place of refuge. |
| 中文 | 敬畏耶和华的，大有依靠。他的儿女，也有避难所。 |

## 27

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The fear of the LORD is a fountain of life, to depart from the snares of death. |
| 中文 | 敬畏耶和华，就是生命的泉源，可以使人离开死亡的网罗。 |

## 28

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | In the multitude of people is the king's honour: but in the want of people is the destruction of the prince. |
| 中文 | 帝王荣耀在乎民多。君王衰败在乎民少。 |

## 29

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that is slow to wrath is of great understanding: but he that is hasty of spirit exalteth folly. |
| 中文 | 不轻易发怒的，大有聪明。性情暴躁的，大显愚妄。 |

## 30

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A sound heart is the life of the flesh: but envy the rottenness of the bones. |
| 中文 | 心中安静，是肉体的生命。嫉妒是骨中的朽烂。 |

## 31

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that oppresseth the poor reproacheth his Maker: but he that honoureth him hath mercy on the poor. |
| 中文 | 欺压贫寒的，是辱没造他的主。怜悯穷乏的，乃是尊敬主 |

## 32

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The wicked is driven away in his wickedness: but the righteous hath hope in his death. |
| 中文 | 恶人在所行的恶上，必被推倒。义人临死，有所投靠。 |

## 33

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wisdom resteth in the heart of him that hath understanding: but that which is in the midst of fools is made known. |
| 中文 | 智慧存在聪明人心中。愚昧人心里所存的，显而易见。 |

## 34

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Righteousness exalteth a nation: but sin is a reproach to any people. |
| 中文 | 公义使邦国高举。罪恶是人民的羞辱。 |

## 35

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The king's favour is toward a wise servant: but his wrath is against him that causeth shame. |
| 中文 | 智慧的臣子，蒙王恩惠。贻羞的仆人，遭其震怒。 |
