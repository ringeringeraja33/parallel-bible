# Job 31 / 约伯记 31

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I made a covenant with mine eyes; why then should I think upon a maid? |
| 中文 | 我与眼睛立约，怎能恋恋瞻望处女呢。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For what portion of God is there from above? and what inheritance of the Almighty from on high? |
| 中文 | 从至上的神所得之分，从至高全能者所得之业，是什么呢。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Is not destruction to the wicked? and a strange punishment to the workers of iniquity? |
| 中文 | 岂不是祸患临到不义的，灾害临到作孽的呢。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Doth not he see my ways, and count all my steps? |
| 中文 | 神岂不是察看我的道路，数点我的脚步呢。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I have walked with vanity, or if my foot hath hasted to deceit; |
| 中文 | 我若与虚谎同行，脚若追随诡诈。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Let me be weighed in an even balance that God may know mine integrity. |
| 中文 | 我若被公道的天平称度，使神可以知道我的纯正。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If my step hath turned out of the way, and mine heart walked after mine eyes, and if any blot hath cleaved to mine hands; |
| 中文 | 我的脚步若偏离正路，我的心若随着我的眼目，若有玷污粘在我手上。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Then let me sow, and let another eat; yea, let my offspring be rooted out. |
| 中文 | 就愿我所种的有别人吃，我田所产的被拔出来。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If mine heart have been deceived by a woman, or if I have laid wait at my neighbour's door; |
| 中文 | 我若受迷惑，向妇人起淫念，在邻舍的门外蹲伏。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Then let my wife grind unto another, and let others bow down upon her. |
| 中文 | 就愿我的妻子给别人推磨，别人也与她同室。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For this is an heinous crime; yea, it is an iniquity to be punished by the judges. |
| 中文 | 因为这是大罪，是审判官当罚的罪孽。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For it is a fire that consumeth to destruction, and would root out all mine increase. |
| 中文 | 这本是火焚烧，直到毁灭，必拔除我所有的家产。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I did despise the cause of my manservant or of my maidservant, when they contended with me; |
| 中文 | 我的仆婢与我争辩的时候，我若藐视不听他们的情节。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | What then shall I do when God riseth up? and when he visiteth, what shall I answer him? |
| 中文 | 神兴起，我怎样行呢。他察问，我怎样回答呢。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Did not he that made me in the womb make him? and did not one fashion us in the womb? |
| 中文 | 造我在腹中的，不也是造他吗。将他与我抟在腹中的，岂不是一位吗。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I have withheld the poor from their desire, or have caused the eyes of the widow to fail; |
| 中文 | 我若不容贫寒人得其所愿，或叫寡妇眼中失望， |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Or have eaten my morsel myself alone, and the fatherless hath not eaten thereof; |
| 中文 | 或独自吃我一点食物，孤儿没有与我同吃。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | (For from my youth he was brought up with me, as with a father, and I have guided her from my mother's womb;) |
| 中文 | （从幼年时孤儿与我同长，好像父子一样。我从出母腹就扶助寡妇）。（扶助原文作引领） |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I have seen any perish for want of clothing, or any poor without covering; |
| 中文 | 我若见人因无衣死亡，或见穷乏人身无遮盖。 |

## 20

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If his loins have not blessed me, and if he were not warmed with the fleece of my sheep; |
| 中文 | 我若不使他因我羊的毛得暖，为我祝福。 |

## 21

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I have lifted up my hand against the fatherless, when I saw my help in the gate: |
| 中文 | 我若在城门口见有帮助我的，举手攻击孤儿。 |

## 22

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Then let mine arm fall from my shoulder blade, and mine arm be broken from the bone. |
| 中文 | 情愿我的肩头从缺盆骨脱落，我的膀臂从羊矢骨折断。 |

## 23

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For destruction from God was a terror to me, and by reason of his highness I could not endure. |
| 中文 | 因神降的灾祸使我恐惧。因他的威严，我不能妄为。 |

## 24

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I have made gold my hope, or have said to the fine gold, Thou art my confidence; |
| 中文 | 我若以黄金为指望，对精金说，你是我的倚靠。 |

## 25

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I rejoiced because my wealth was great, and because mine hand had gotten much; |
| 中文 | 我若因财物丰裕，因我手多得资财而欢喜。 |

## 26

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I beheld the sun when it shined, or the moon walking in brightness; |
| 中文 | 我若见太阳发光，明月行在空中， |

## 27

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | And my heart hath been secretly enticed, or my mouth hath kissed my hand: |
| 中文 | 心就暗暗被引诱，口便亲手。 |

## 28

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | This also were an iniquity to be punished by the judge: for I should have denied the God that is above. |
| 中文 | 这也是审判官当罚的罪孽，又是我背弃在上的神。 |

## 29

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I rejoiced at the destruction of him that hated me, or lifted up myself when evil found him: |
| 中文 | 我若见恨我的遭报就欢喜，见他遭灾便高兴。 |

## 30

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Neither have I suffered my mouth to sin by wishing a curse to his soul. |
| 中文 | （我没有容口犯罪，咒诅他的生命） |

## 31

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If the men of my tabernacle said not, Oh that we had of his flesh! we cannot be satisfied. |
| 中文 | 若我帐棚的人未尝说，谁不以主人的食物吃饱呢。 |

## 32

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The stranger did not lodge in the street: but I opened my doors to the traveller. |
| 中文 | （从来我没有容客旅在街上住宿，却开门迎接行路的人） |

## 33

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I covered my transgressions as Adam, by hiding mine iniquity in my bosom: |
| 中文 | 我若像亚当（或作别人）遮掩我的过犯，将罪孽藏在怀中。 |

## 34

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Did I fear a great multitude, or did the contempt of families terrify me, that I kept silence, and went not out of the door? |
| 中文 | 因惧怕大众，又因宗族藐视我使我惊恐，以致闭口无言，杜门不出。 |

## 35

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Oh that one would hear me! behold, my desire is, that the Almighty would answer me, and that mine adversary had written a book. |
| 中文 | 惟愿有一位肯听我。（看哪，在这里有我所划的押，愿全能者回答我） |

## 36

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Surely I would take it upon my shoulder, and bind it as a crown to me. |
| 中文 | 愿那敌我者，所写的状词在我这里，我必带在肩上，又绑在头上为冠冕。 |

## 37

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I would declare unto him the number of my steps; as a prince would I go near unto him. |
| 中文 | 我必向他述说我脚步的数目，必如君王进到他面前。 |

## 38

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If my land cry against me, or that the furrows likewise thereof complain; |
| 中文 | 我若夺取田地，这地向我喊冤，犁沟一同哭泣。 |

## 39

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, or have caused the owners thereof to lose their life: |
| 中文 | 我若吃地的出产不给价值，或叫原主丧命。 |

## 40

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |

| 中文 | 愿这地长蒺藜代替麦子，长恶草代替大麦。约伯的话说完了。 |
