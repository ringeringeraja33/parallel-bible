# Psalms 49

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Hear this, all ye people; give ear, all ye inhabitants of the world: |
| 中文 | （可拉后裔的诗，交与伶长）万民哪，你们都当听这话，世上一切的居民， |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Both low and high, rich and poor, together. |
| 中文 | 无论上流下流，富足贫穷，都当留心听。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | My mouth shall speak of wisdom; and the meditation of my heart shall be of understanding. |
| 中文 | 我口要说智慧的言语。我必要想通达的道理。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I will incline mine ear to a parable: I will open my dark saying upon the harp. |
| 中文 | 我要侧耳听比喻，用琴解谜语。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wherefore should I fear in the days of evil, when the iniquity of my heels shall compass me about? |
| 中文 | 在患难的日子，奸恶随我脚跟，四面环绕我，我何必惧怕。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | They that trust in their wealth, and boast themselves in the multitude of their riches; |
| 中文 | 那些倚仗财货自夸钱财多的人， |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | None of them can by any means redeem his brother, nor give to God a ransom for him: |
| 中文 | 一个也无法赎自己的弟兄，也不能替他将赎价给神， |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | (For the redemption of their soul is precious, and it ceaseth for ever:) |
| 中文 | 叫他长远活着，不见朽坏。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | That he should still live for ever, and not see corruption. |
| 中文 | 因为赎他生命的价值极贵，只可永远吧休。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For he seeth that wise men die, likewise the fool and the brutish person perish, and leave their wealth to others. |
| 中文 | 他必见智慧人死。又见愚顽人和畜类人，一同灭亡，将他们的财货留给别人。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Their inward thought is, that their houses shall continue for ever, and their dwelling places to all generations; they call their lands after their own names. |
| 中文 | 他们心里思想，他们的家室必永存，住宅必留到万代。他们以自己的名，称自己的地。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Nevertheless man being in honour abideth not: he is like the beasts that perish. |
| 中文 | 但人居尊贵中不能长久，如同死亡的畜类一样。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | This their way is their folly: yet their posterity approve their sayings. Selah. |
| 中文 | 他们行的这道，本为自己的愚昧。但他们以后的人，还佩服他们的话语。（细拉）。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Like sheep they are laid in the grave; death shall feed on them; and the upright shall have dominion over them in the morning; and their beauty shall consume in the grave from their dwelling. |
| 中文 | 他们如同羊群派定下阴间。死亡必作他们的牧者。到了早晨，正直人必管辖他们。他们的美容，必被阴间所灭，以致无处可存。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | But God will redeem my soul from the power of the grave: for he shall receive me. Selah. |
| 中文 | 只是神必救赎我的灵魂，脱离阴间的权柄，因他必收纳我。（细拉） |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Be not thou afraid when one is made rich, when the glory of his house is increased; |
| 中文 | 见人发财家室增荣的时候，你不要惧怕。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For when he dieth he shall carry nothing away: his glory shall not descend after him. |
| 中文 | 因为他死的时候，什么也不能带去。他的荣耀不能随他下去。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Though while he lived he blessed his soul: and men will praise thee, when thou doest well to thyself. |
| 中文 | 他活着的时候，虽然自夸为有福，（你若利己，人必夸奖你）。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He shall go to the generation of his fathers; they shall never see light. |
| 中文 | 他仍必归到他历代的祖宗那里，永不见光。 |

## 20

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Man that is in honour, and understandeth not, is like the beasts that perish. |
| 中文 | 人在尊贵中，而不醒悟，就如死亡的畜类一样。 |
