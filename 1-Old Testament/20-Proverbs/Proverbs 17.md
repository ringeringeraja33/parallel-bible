# Proverbs 17 / 箴言 17

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Better is a dry morsel, and quietness therewith, than an house full of sacrifices with strife. |
| 中文 | 设筵满屋，大家相争，不如有块乾饼，大家相安。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A wise servant shall have rule over a son that causeth shame, and shall have part of the inheritance among the brethren. |
| 中文 | 仆人办事聪明，必管辖贻羞之子，又在众子中，同分产业。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The fining pot is for silver, and the furnace for gold: but the LORD trieth the hearts. |
| 中文 | 鼎为炼银，炉为炼金。惟有耶和华熬炼人心。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A wicked doer giveth heed to false lips; and a liar giveth ear to a naughty tongue. |
| 中文 | 行恶的留心听奸诈之言。说谎的侧耳听邪恶之语。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Whoso mocketh the poor reproacheth his Maker: and he that is glad at calamities shall not be unpunished. |
| 中文 | 戏笑穷人的，是辱没造他的主。幸灾乐祸的，必不免受罚。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Children's children are the crown of old men; and the glory of children are their fathers. |
| 中文 | 子孙为老人的冠冕。父亲是儿女的荣耀。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Excellent speech becometh not a fool: much less do lying lips a prince. |
| 中文 | 愚顽人说美言本不相宜，何况君王说谎话呢。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A gift is as a precious stone in the eyes of him that hath it: whithersoever it turneth, it prospereth. |
| 中文 | 贿赂在馈送人的眼中，看为宝玉。随处运动，都得顺利。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that covereth a transgression seeketh love; but he that repeateth a matter separateth very friends. |
| 中文 | 遮掩人过的，寻求人爱。屡次挑错的，离间密友。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A reproof entereth more into a wise man than an hundred stripes into a fool. |
| 中文 | 一句责备话，深入聪明人的心，强如责打愚昧人一百下。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | An evil man seeketh only rebellion: therefore a cruel messenger shall be sent against him. |
| 中文 | 恶人只寻背叛，所以必有严厉的使者，奉差攻击他。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Let a bear robbed of her whelps meet a man, rather than a fool in his folly. |
| 中文 | 宁可遇见丢崽子的母熊，不可遇见正行愚妄的愚昧人。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Whoso rewardeth evil for good, evil shall not depart from his house. |
| 中文 | 以恶报善的，祸患必不离他的家。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The beginning of strife is as when one letteth out water: therefore leave off contention, before it be meddled with. |
| 中文 | 分争的起头，如水放开。所以在争闹之先，必当止息争竞。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He that justifieth the wicked, and he that condemneth the just, even they both are abomination to the LORD. |
| 中文 | 定恶人为义的，定义人为恶的，这都为耶和华所憎恶。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wherefore is there a price in the hand of a fool to get wisdom, seeing he hath no heart to it? |
| 中文 | 愚昧人既无聪明，为何手拿价银买智慧呢。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A friend loveth at all times, and a brother is born for adversity. |
| 中文 | 朋友乃时常亲爱。弟兄为患难而生。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |

| 中文 | 在邻舍面前击掌作保，乃是无知的人。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |

## 20

| --- | --- |

| 中文 | 心存邪僻的，寻不着好处。舌弄是非的，陷在祸患中。 |
