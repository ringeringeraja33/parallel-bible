# Song of Songs 2

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I am the rose of Sharon, and the lily of the valleys. |
| 中文 | 我是沙仑的玫瑰花（或作水仙花），是谷中的百合花。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As the lily among thorns, so is my love among the daughters. |
| 中文 | 我的佳偶在女子中，好像百合花在荆棘内。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | As the apple tree among the trees of the wood, so is my beloved among the sons. I sat down under his shadow with great delight, and his fruit was sweet to my taste. |
| 中文 | 我的良人在男子中，如同苹果树在树林中。我欢欢喜喜坐在他的荫下，尝他果子的滋味，觉得甘甜。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | He brought me to the banqueting house, and his banner over me was love. |
| 中文 | 他带我入筵宴所，以爱为旗在我以上。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Stay me with flagons, comfort me with apples: for I am sick of love. |
| 中文 | 求你们给我葡萄乾增补我力，给我苹果畅快我心，因我思爱成病。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | His left hand is under my head, and his right hand doth embrace me. |
| 中文 | 他的左手在我头下，他的右手将我抱住。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I charge you, O ye daughters of Jerusalem, by the roes, and by the hinds of the field, that ye stir not up, nor awake my love, till he please. |
| 中文 | 耶路撒冷的众女子阿，我指着羚羊或田野的母鹿，嘱咐你们，不要惊动，不要叫醒我所亲爱的，等他自己情愿（不要叫醒云云或作不要激动爱情等它自发）。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The voice of my beloved! behold, he cometh leaping upon the mountains, skipping upon the hills. |
| 中文 | 听阿，是我良人的声音。看哪，他蹿山越岭而来。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | My beloved is like a roe or a young hart: behold, he standeth behind our wall, he looketh forth at the windows, shewing himself through the lattice. |
| 中文 | 我的良人好像羚羊，或像小鹿。他站在我们墙壁后，从窗户往里观看，从窗棂往里窥探。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | My beloved spake, and said unto me, Rise up, my love, my fair one, and come away. |
| 中文 | 我良人对我说，我的佳偶，我的美人，起来，与我同去。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For, lo, the winter is past, the rain is over and gone; |
| 中文 | 因为冬天已往。雨水止住过去了。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The flowers appear on the earth; the time of the singing of birds is come, and the voice of the turtle is heard in our land; |
| 中文 | 地上百花开放。百鸟呜叫的时候（或作修理葡萄树的时候）已经来到，斑鸠的声音在我们境内也听见了。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The fig tree putteth forth her green figs, and the vines with the tender grape give a good smell. Arise, my love, my fair one, and come away. |
| 中文 | 无花果树的果子渐渐成熟，葡萄树开花放香。我的佳偶，我的美人，起来，与我同去。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | O my dove, that art in the clefts of the rock, in the secret places of the stairs, let me see thy countenance, let me hear thy voice; for sweet is thy voice, and thy countenance is comely. |
| 中文 | 我的鸽子阿，你在磐石穴中，在陡岩的隐密处。求你容我得见你的面貌，得听你的声音。因为你的声音柔和，你的面貌秀美。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Take us the foxes, the little foxes, that spoil the vines: for our vines have tender grapes. |
| 中文 | 要给我们擒拿狐狸，就是毁坏葡萄园的小狐狸。因为我们的葡萄正在开花。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | My beloved is mine, and I am his: he feedeth among the lilies. |
| 中文 | 良人属我，我也属他。他在百合花中牧放群羊 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Until the day break, and the shadows flee away, turn, my beloved, and be thou like a roe or a young hart upon the mountains of Bether. |
| 中文 | 我的良人哪，求你等到天起凉风，日影飞去的时候，你要转回，好像羚羊，或像小鹿在比特山上。 |
