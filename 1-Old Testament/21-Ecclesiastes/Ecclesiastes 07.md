# Ecclesiastes 7

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | A good name is better than precious ointment; and the day of death than the day of one's birth. |
| 中文 | 名誉强如美好的膏油。人死的日子，胜过人生的日子。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | It is better to go to the house of mourning, than to go to the house of feasting: for that is the end of all men; and the living will lay it to his heart. |
| 中文 | 往遭丧的家去，强如往宴乐的家去，因为死是众人的结局。活人也必将这事放在心上。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Sorrow is better than laughter: for by the sadness of the countenance the heart is made better. |
| 中文 | 忧愁强如喜笑，因为面带愁容，终必使心喜乐。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The heart of the wise is in the house of mourning; but the heart of fools is in the house of mirth. |
| 中文 | 智慧人的心，在遭丧之家。愚昧人的心，在快乐之家。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | It is better to hear the rebuke of the wise, than for a man to hear the song of fools. |
| 中文 | 听智慧人的责备，强如听愚昧人的歌唱。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For as the crackling of thorns under a pot, so is the laughter of the fool: this also is vanity. |
| 中文 | 愚昧人的笑声，好像锅下烧荆棘的爆声，这也是虚空。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Surely oppression maketh a wise man mad; and a gift destroyeth the heart. |
| 中文 | 勒索使智慧人变为愚妄。贿赂能败坏人的慧心。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Better is the end of a thing than the beginning thereof: and the patient in spirit is better than the proud in spirit. |
| 中文 | 事情的终局，强如事情的起头。存心忍耐的，胜过居心骄傲的。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Be not hasty in thy spirit to be angry: for anger resteth in the bosom of fools. |
| 中文 | 你不要心里急躁恼怒，因为恼怒存在愚昧人的怀中。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Say not thou, What is the cause that the former days were better than these? for thou dost not enquire wisely concerning this. |
| 中文 | 不要说，先前的日子强过如今的日子，是什么缘故呢。你这样问，不是出于智慧。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wisdom is good with an inheritance: and by it there is profit to them that see the sun. |
| 中文 | 智慧和产业并好。而且见天日的人，得智慧更为有益。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For wisdom is a defence, and money is a defence: but the excellency of knowledge is, that wisdom giveth life to them that have it. |
| 中文 | 因为智慧护庇人，好像银钱护庇人一样。惟独智慧能保全智慧人的生命，这就是知识的益处。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Consider the work of God: for who can make that straight, which he hath made crooked? |
| 中文 | 你要察看神的作为，因为神使为曲的，谁能变为直呢。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | In the day of prosperity be joyful, but in the day of adversity consider: God also hath set the one over against the other, to the end that man should find nothing after him. |
| 中文 | 遇亨通的日子，你当喜乐。遭患难的日子，你当思想。因为神使这两样并列，为的是叫人查不出身后有什么事。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | All things have I seen in the days of my vanity: there is a just man that perisheth in his righteousness, and there is a wicked man that prolongeth his life in his wickedness. |
| 中文 | 有义人行义，反致灭亡。有恶人行恶，倒享长寿。这都是我在虚度之日中所见过的。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Be not righteous over much; neither make thyself over wise: why shouldest thou destroy thyself ? |
| 中文 | 不要行义过分。也不要过于自逞智慧。何必自取败亡呢。 |

## 17

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Be not over much wicked, neither be thou foolish: why shouldest thou die before thy time? |
| 中文 | 不要行恶过分。也不要为人愚昧。何必不到期而死呢。 |

## 18

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | It is good that thou shouldest take hold of this; yea, also from this withdraw not thine hand: for he that feareth God shall come forth of them all. |
| 中文 | 你持守这个为美。那个也不要松手。因为敬畏神的人，必从这两样出来。 |

## 19

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wisdom strengtheneth the wise more than ten mighty men which are in the city. |
| 中文 | 智慧使有智慧的人，比城中十个官长更有能力。 |

## 20

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For there is not a just man upon earth, that doeth good, and sinneth not. |
| 中文 | 时常行善而不犯罪的义人，世上实在没有。 |

## 21

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Also take no heed unto all words that are spoken; lest thou hear thy servant curse thee: |
| 中文 | 人所说的一切话，你不要放在心上，恐怕听见你的仆人咒诅你。 |

## 22

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For oftentimes also thine own heart knoweth that thou thyself likewise hast cursed others. |
| 中文 | 因为你心里知道，自己也曾屡次咒诅别人。 |

## 23

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | All this have I proved by wisdom: I said, I will be wise; but it was far from me. |
| 中文 | 我曾用智慧试验这一切事。我说，要得智慧，智慧却离我远。 |

## 24

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | That which is far off, and exceeding deep, who can find it out? |
| 中文 | 万事之理离我甚远，而且最深，谁能测透呢。 |

## 25

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I applied mine heart to know, and to search, and to seek out wisdom, and the reason of things, and to know the wickedness of folly, even of foolishness and madness: |
| 中文 | 我转念，一心要知道，要考察，要寻求智慧，和万事的理由。又要知道邪恶为愚昧，愚昧为狂妄。 |

## 26

| 中文 | 有罪的人，却被她缠住了。 |
