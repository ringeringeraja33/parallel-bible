# Ecclesiastes 4 / 传道书 4

## 1

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | So I returned, and considered all the oppressions that are done under the sun: and behold the tears of such as were oppressed, and they had no comforter; and on the side of their oppressors there was power; but they had no comforter. |
| 中文 | 我又转念，见日光之下所行的一切欺压。看哪，受欺压的流泪，且无人安慰。欺压他们的有势力，也无人安慰他们。 |

## 2

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Wherefore I praised the dead which are already dead more than the living which are yet alive. |
| 中文 | 因此，我赞叹那早已死的死人，胜过那还活着的活人。 |

## 3

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Yea, better is he than both they, which hath not yet been, who hath not seen the evil work that is done under the sun. |
| 中文 | 并且我以为那未曾生的，就是未见过日光之下恶事的，比这两等人更强。 |

## 4

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Again, I considered all travail, and every right work, that for this a man is envied of his neighbour. This is also vanity and vexation of spirit. |
| 中文 | 我又见人为一切的劳碌，和各样灵巧的工作，就被邻舍嫉妒。这也是虚空，也是捕风。 |

## 5

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | The fool foldeth his hands together, and eateth his own flesh. |
| 中文 | 愚昧人抱着手，吃自己的肉。 |

## 6

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Better is an handful with quietness, than both the hands full with travail and vexation of spirit. |
| 中文 | 满了一把，得享安静，强如满了两把，劳碌捕风。 |

## 7

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Then I returned, and I saw vanity under the sun. |
| 中文 | 我又转念，见日光之下有一件虚空的事。 |

## 8

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | There is one alone, and there is not a second; yea, he hath neither child nor brother: yet is there no end of all his labour; neither is his eye satisfied with riches; neither saith he, For whom do I labour, and bereave my soul of good? This is also vanity, yea, it is a sore travail. |
| 中文 | 有人孤单无二，无子，无兄，竟劳碌不息，眼目也不以钱财为足。（他说）我劳劳碌碌，刻苦自己，不享福乐，到底是为谁呢。这也是虚空，是极重的劳苦。 |

## 9

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Two are better than one; because they have a good reward for their labour. |
| 中文 | 两个人总比一个人好，因为二人劳碌同得美好的果效。 |

## 10

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him that is alone when he falleth; for he hath not another to help him up. |
| 中文 | 若是跌倒，这人可以扶起他的同伴。若是孤身跌倒，没有别人扶起他来。这人就有祸了。 |

## 11

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Again, if two lie together, then they have heat: but how can one be warm alone? |
| 中文 | 再者，二人同睡，就都暖和。一人独睡，怎能暖和呢。 |

## 12

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | And if one prevail against him, two shall withstand him; and a threefold cord is not quickly broken. |
| 中文 | 有人功胜孤身一人，若有二人便能敌挡他。三股合成的绳子，不容易折断。 |

## 13

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | Better is a poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be admonished. |
| 中文 | 贫穷而有智慧的少年人，胜过年老不肯纳谏的愚昧王。 |

## 14

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | For out of prison he cometh to reign; whereas also he that is born in his kingdom becometh poor. |
| 中文 | 这人是从监牢中出来作王。在他国中，生来原是贫穷的。 |

## 15

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | I considered all the living which walk under the sun, with the second child that shall stand up in his stead. |
| 中文 | 我见日光之下一切行动的活人，都随从那第二位，就是起来替老王的少年人。 |

## 16

| Language | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English | There is no end of all the people, even of all that have been before them: they also that come after shall not rejoice in him. Surely this also is vanity and vexation of spirit. |
| 中文 | 他所治理的众人，就是他的百姓，多得无数。在他后来的人，尚且不喜悦他。这是虚空，也是捕风。 |
